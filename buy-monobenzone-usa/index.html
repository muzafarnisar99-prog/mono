How Monobenzone Cream Works for Vitiligo

Monobenzone (MBEH — monobenzyl ether of hydroquinone) is the only FDA-approved treatment for permanent depigmentation in extensive vitiligo. It works through two proven mechanisms:

🔬 Mechanism 1
Free Radical Formation

Monobenzone is oxidized by tyrosinase inside melanocytes, generating reactive quinone free radicals that permanently destroy the pigment-producing cells.

⚗️ Mechanism 2
Tyrosinase Inhibition

Simultaneously blocks the key enzyme responsible for melanin production, stopping new pigment from forming while destroying existing melanocytes.

The result is gradual, permanent lightening of treated skin areas to match the natural white of vitiligo patches — creating a uniform skin appearance. Unlike hydroquinone which only temporarily suppresses pigment, monobenzone's effect is irreversible because it permanently destroys melanocytes.

📚 Clinical reference: Rordam OM et al. "Successful Treatment of Extensive Vitiligo with Monobenzone." Journal of Clinical and Aesthetic Dermatology. 2012. PMID: PMC3533321 — documented extremely satisfactory permanent depigmentation within 3.5 months, stable at 3-year follow-up.

Who Should Use Monobenzone Cream?

According to dermatological guidelines, monobenzone cream is appropriate for:

  • Patients with vitiligo affecting more than 50% of body surface area
  • Patients for whom repigmentation therapy (steroids, tacrolimus, NB-UVB, PUVA) has failed over 12+ months
  • Patients who have made an informed, permanent decision about depigmentation
  • Adults under care of a qualified US dermatologist
⚠️ Who should NOT use monobenzone: Do not use for cosmetic skin lightening. Do not use if pregnant or breastfeeding without specific medical guidance. Do not use on children under 12. Effects are permanent and cannot be reversed. Always consult a board-certified US dermatologist before starting.
